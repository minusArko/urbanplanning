What Is Climate Change?

Climate change stems from human activities like burning fossil fuels and deforestation, causing a surge in greenhouse gases. This leads to rising temperatures, changed weather patterns, and environmental disruptions, including severe heatwaves, extreme weather events, rising sea levels, and threats to ecosystems. Mitigating climate change requires reducing carbon emissions, adopting sustainable practices, and fostering global cooperation to tackle this urgent and interconnected challenge.

Impacts of Climate Change

The history of our planet have been characterized by frequent changes in temperatures (ie. ice ages). Howver, since the Industrial Revolution in the 20th century, global temperatures have risen by 0.6 C. Even slight deviations in temperature can result in vast changes in the environment and weather. For instance, if emission trends continue, global sea levels will rise from 0.09 to 0.88 meters by the start of the next century. Why might this matter you may ask? Millions of civilians living in coastal cities will be forced to relocate. Not only will this affect many people, places of rich biodiversity such as the Florida Everglades will be flooded causing massive implications to the ecosystem.
