What Is Climate Change?

Climate change stems from human activities like burning fossil fuels and deforestation, causing a surge in greenhouse gases. This leads to rising temperatures, changed weather patterns, and environmental disruptions, including severe heatwaves, extreme weather events, rising sea levels, and threats to ecosystems. Mitigating climate change requires reducing carbon emissions, adopting sustainable practices, and fostering global cooperation to tackle this urgent and interconnected challenge.

Impacts of Climate Change

The history of our planet have been characterized by frequent changes in temperatures (ie. ice ages). Howver, since the Industrial Revolution in the 20th century, global temperatures have risen by 0.6 C. Even slight deviations in temperature can result in vast changes in the environment and weather. For instance, if emission trends continue, global sea levels will rise from 0.09 to 0.88 meters by the start of the next century. Why might this matter you may ask? Millions of civilians living in coastal cities will be forced to relocate. Not only will this affect many people, places of rich biodiversity such as the Florida Everglades will be flooded causing massive implications to the ecosystem.

What is Urban Planning?

Urban planning is the strategic organization and development of land in cities to create sustainable, functional, and visually appealing environments. It involves crafting policies and strategies to guide the growth of urban areas, considering factors like transportation, housing, infrastructure, and public spaces. A city with good urban planning and design promote long-term sustainability and enhance the overall quality of life for their inhabitants.

Climate Change Mitigation

The major cities and urban areas of the globe contributes substantially to greenhouse emissions. As such, taking sustainability into consideration in urban design and urban planning is crucial in lowering global emissions. There are many design strategies and methods in urban planning that helps with achieving sustainability such as accessible and reliable public transportation, renewable energy, and energy efficency in building. Good Public Transit

One strategy of urban planning achieving sustainability is having reliable public transportion, and encouraging the use of them. Having good transit systems reduces people's need for owning their own vehicles, greatly reducing total greenhouse gas emissions as a typical vehicle with consistent use will produce 4.6 metric tons of carbon dioxide per year. Additionally, public transportation is generally more energy-efficient and can hold, and transport more people, thus using less energy than if each individual had a vehicle. The China Railway High-speed and the Bullet Train in Japan are examples of transit systems that perfectly encapsulates this. Both of them are incredibly fast, energy-efficient, and highly utilized by the city's residents.

Politics and Urban Design

While one may not think of it, urban plannign and design are political in nature. First, the city must have interest in sustainable design, then there must be funding for for the project itself. However, funding must be approved by local governments. Additionally, there are also the more nuanced factors such as interest groups, private investors, and the city planners themselves. Each of these groups have their own goals and interests, and thus achieving good urban design and sustainability requires extensive negotation, and these efforts often end up in vain.

United States
